Why We Built This

We Live Every Day
"in between" appointments.

While managing my ET, I spent nearly two months exhausted. Tired in the morning. Groggy by afternoon. I couldn't think clearly. I assumed it was the disease. I kept going.

I was doing blood testing every six weeks and quarterly appointments. In my appointment we looked at my labs together. My red blood cell count had quietly dropped just below normal. I didn't think anything of it — but it turned out my Hydrea dose was one pill per week too high. We made a simple adjustment, and within weeks I felt like myself again.

Did I need to wait 60+ days? Could I have known earlier? I had all the data. I just didn't understand it.

I was lucky. I see my hemonc every three months. The majority of patients managing chronic cancer see their doctor every six months — or once a year. That's a long time to be in the dark.

Live Every Day is built for the time between appointments — and for making the most of the time you have with your doctor when that day finally comes.

Who It's For

There are millions of us managing cancer between appointments.
Most of us have never seen an app that knows our name.

MPN · Launching first
Essential Thrombocythemia, Polycythemia Vera & Myelofibrosis
Where Live Every Day begins. Built around the MPN-10 symptom instrument, CBC trend monitoring, and the specific questions MPN patients bring to their hematologist — but never have time to ask.
CLL · Coming soon
Chronic Lymphocytic Leukemia & Indolent Lymphoma
Watch and wait can last years. The monitoring never stops, the uncertainty never fully lifts. Live Every Day tracks the full picture — so when something changes, we see it in context.
Prostate · Coming soon
Prostate Cancer — Active Surveillance
Scheduled PSA tests, periodic biopsies, and the ongoing discipline of watching a diagnosis that may never require intervention. Show up to every monitoring appointment with the full longitudinal picture.
Post-treatment · Coming soon
After Treatment — The Watchful Window
Treatment is over. We've been told the best possible news. But the work of understanding our own health isn't finished — it enters a new phase. The months of close monitoring deserve the same tools as any chronic condition.
Thyroid · Coming soon
Thyroid Cancer & Early-Stage Breast Cancer
High survival rates don't mean low attention requirements. Years of hormone management, regular scans, and the ongoing discipline of long-term monitoring deserve the same quality of insight.
Expanding
More conditions on the roadmap
Each condition requires its own validated symptom instrument and specialist clinical advisor. We're building condition by condition — with the depth each one deserves.

The conditions are different. The treatments are different. The timelines are different.

But the experience is the same: bodies that don't pause between appointments, a healthcare system that can only listen a few times a year, and all of us who deserve to understand our own story — not just at the next visit, but every day.
